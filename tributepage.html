Atal Bihari Vajpayee

Introduction

Atal Bihari Vajpayee was an Indian politician, poet, and statesman who served as the Prime Minister of India three times. He was born on December 25, 1924, in Gwalior, Madhya Pradesh, and passed away on August 16, 2018, in New Delhi, India.

Early Life

Vajpayee was the eldest of three children of Krishna Devi and Krishna Bihari Vajpayee. He attended Victoria College (now known as Laxmi Bai College) in Gwalior and later earned his bachelor's degree in Hindi, English, and Sanskrit from DAV College in Kanpur. He went on to pursue a post-graduate degree in political science from the University of Lucknow.

Political Career

Vajpayee's political career began in the 1940s when he joined the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS), a right-wing Hindu nationalist organization. He later became a member of the Bharatiya Jana Sangh (BJS), which was later renamed as the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP). He served as the Prime Minister of India from 1996 to 2004, and his tenure is remembered for the Pokhran-II nuclear tests and the Kargil War.

Legacy

Vajpayee was known for his oratory skills, poetry, and statesmanship. He was awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 2015. His birthday, December 25, is celebrated as Good Governance Day in India. His poems have been published in various anthologies, and he is considered one of the greatest statesmen in Indian history.

Conclusion

Atal Bihari Vajpayee will always be remembered as one of India's greatest leaders. His contributions to Indian politics, literature, and society will continue to inspire generations to come.
